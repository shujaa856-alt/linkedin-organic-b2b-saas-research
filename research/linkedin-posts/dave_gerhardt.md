### Topic

Using reverse psychology to promote a B2B community event.

### Summary

Gerhardt uses a playful, counterintuitive invitation to distinguish Exit Five's annual event from routine conference promotion. The creative framing attracts attention before directing it toward practical benefits for B2B marketers. Those benefits include useful learning, relevant professional relationships, and an enjoyable shared experience. B2B SaaS teams can apply this approach by pairing an unexpected hook with clear audience value.

### Key Takeaways

- Use an unexpected opening to interrupt familiar feed patterns.
- Connect creative promotion to specific attendee benefits.
- Let event messaging demonstrate the experience and personality on offer.

## Post 2

**Date:** June 17, 2026

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_spoiler-alert-everything-in-marketing-works-activity-7472966743162945537-oz8X?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAACgFeLQB8mrZa5tsU5IVzlAOu5SUAe5K75A

### Topic

Focused channel selection and committed marketing execution.

### Summary

Gerhardt argues that many established marketing channels can succeed under the right conditions. Performance depends on selecting channels suited to the business and executing them with strategic depth. Attempting every available tactic spreads attention too thinly to develop meaningful competence. B2B SaaS marketers should favor focused commitment and iteration over constant channel switching.

### Key Takeaways

- Select channels based on buyers, resources, and business goals.
- Concentrate effort on a small set of strategic priorities.
- Allow time for learning and compounding before changing direction.

## Post 3

**Date:** June 14, 2026

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_when-i-say-were-building-the-top-community-activity-7470791967061381121-6ijx?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAACgFeLQB8mrZa5tsU5IVzlAOu5SUAe5K75A

### Topic

Measuring community success through impact and advocacy.

### Summary

Gerhardt frames community quality as something demonstrated by members rather than declared by its operator. Growth figures show reach but do not establish whether participation creates meaningful value. Member outcomes and voluntary recommendations offer stronger evidence of trust and usefulness. B2B SaaS communities should therefore assess depth of impact alongside acquisition metrics.

### Key Takeaways

- Track useful member outcomes in addition to membership growth.
- Treat unsolicited referrals as a signal of community health.
- Build repeatable opportunities for members to learn and connect.
